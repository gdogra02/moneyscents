How to Use a Debit Card Responsibly


May 31, 2017 by Arif Aulakh


Many high school and university students use debit cards for purchases instead of withdrawing cash from their bank accounts. But while a debit card is a convenient and immediate way to pay, there are some important tips that should be kept in mind for using the card responsibly before using it regularly. As a student myself, these are things which I have come to realize after my own experience with using a debit card.


1. Check your account regularly

The first important thing to do when using a debit card is to keep track of how much you’re spending, ideally once every few days or after a large series of purchases. Always be sure to check your account summary directly. Many people, including myself, attempt to add up the numbers in their head rather than checking their account, which can lead to much more liberal estimates and thinking there is more money than what exists in reality. To take things one step further, use your account summary to make a comprehensive budget for yourself.


2. Ensure your card is protected

The next important thing to look over is the security of your debit card. Before, the swiping of debit cards posed a significant security risk. Now, with chip and pin and even tap technology, cards are much more secure and much more convenient. However, if these newer debit cards are lost, it is imperative that you let your bank know as soon as possible since thieves are able to use the card too.


3. Use your bank's ATM

While debit cards do provide much convenience, there are still times when students need to use cash. In these cases, students will most likely have to use an ATM to withdraw money. It is imperative to try to use ATMs that belong to your bank, in order to avoid the unnecessary fees. While a $3 fee may not feel like very much the first time, it begins to start adding up over time, and can lead to the loss of money unnecessarily.


4. Be aware of which type of account you are using

The last main tip regarding the responsible use of debit cards is to make sure you know which account in your debit you are using. Some accounts charge you fees for every debit card transaction while others, such as student accounts, give you a number of transactions for free. In addition, because bank users can open and put money in both chequing and savings accounts, it is important to make sure you are withdrawing from the right one when purchasing something. Having this knowledge will be better for understanding how much you are spending and how much you have, along with being able to budget effectively. Not knowing such information can lead to the same problems which I discussed in my first tip such as overspending and running out of money.


In conclusion, debit cards are a very important resource when gaining full financial independence. However, they need to be used responsibly, or otherwise problems can arise such as overspending, theft, or paying high fees.
