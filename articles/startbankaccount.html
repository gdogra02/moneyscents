Things You Should Know Before Setting up a Bank Account


July 22, 2017 by Tiffany Tran


So you’ve finally decided that you want to open a bank account. Maybe you’ve just landed yourself your first job and want to start saving for the future or maybe you want to try a new way of managing your money. Regardless of the reason, congratulations on taking your first step in building a solid financial future!


Here are three things you should know prior to setting up a bank account.


1. What exactly is a bank account?

By definition, a bank account is any type of account offered by a financial institution where you can withdraw or deposit money. Depending on the type, you may even be able to earn interest on the money you have in the account.


There are many different types of bank accounts: basic checking accounts, certificates of deposit, interest-breaking checking accounts, money market deposit accounts, and savings accounts. We will go more in depth into a few of these later on.



2. Which financial institution is right for me?

When deciding where you want to put your money, convenience should be your top priority. Choosing an institution too far away from your home or work could start giving you unnecessary high gas and ATM bills.


There are several institutions where you can open a bank account. Banks, credit unions, and trust companies are just a few of the many that are available. It is important to do some research on the financial institutions near you and see what they offer.


It is also essential that you understand the terms and conditions set by the financial institution before you open an account. No one likes to read the terms and conditions, I know, but there are usually hidden fees and surcharges written in the fine print that you would not know about otherwise.


3. What type of bank account am I looking for?

As previously mentioned, there are several types of bank accounts you can consider. Two of the most common are checking accounts and savings accounts.


Checking account: A checking account is used mainly in your day-to-day life. Think of it as a temporary storage place. You deposit money into the account and take it out when using a debit card or writing cheques as required. As you can see, money in this account is constantly coming in or going out and usually does not stay for long.


Savings account: A savings account on the other hand is meant for long-term use. It is a place for you to save your money. The money you put in stays in the account for a long period of time. The good news is, money in this account can grow depending on your interest rate. Putting money in here is like being paid without doing anything. You even get to save money for the future! How cool is that?



As always, you should choose a bank account that suits your needs and requirements.


I’m ready - What now?

You now have the information you need to start managing and saving your money. So, what are you waiting for? Head over to the financial institution of your choice with your social security number and driver’s license and open your bank account!
