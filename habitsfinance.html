Which Habits are the Key to Financial Success


August 7, 2017 by Harsh Chahal


Money is necessary to achieve many goals in your life. Whether you want to go to college, buy a new car or get a mortgage. But many people believe that solely getting a job can help you achieve these goals, instead, a job is only part of the equation to financial success. We all revolve around habits even if we may never notice them being there. Building good financial habits is the path to ensure you’ll have the money you need, when you need it.


Course of Action

As a teenager, I would constantly get confused with all the different ways I could invest my money from stocks, bonds, mutual funds and even crypto currencies. Investing may not be a walk in the park, but it isn't rocket science either. Before doing anything, you must make a PLAN. Begin by setting a goal for yourself such as: you want to save $500,000 by the time you’re 75 or you want a certain amount of return on your investments before retirement. Next, choose your investment strategy which will help you achieve your goals. If you are saving for long-term goals, you might choose a more low-risk investment, or a more aggressive high-risk investment for your short-term goals. Lastly, you have to stick to your plan and never divert away from it even if you aren't getting the returns you want. Simply improve your plan don't make a new one.


Be a Super Saver

Most people, including myself, have a careless need for spending money on things we don't really need. Saving money is worth the effort. Getting into the habit of saving gives you a peace of mind, it gives you options, and the more you save the easier it becomes to accumulate additional savings. Get comfortable with preparing a budget. This will help you identify potential areas for reducing expenses and ensure you know where your money is being spent. The money left over can be saved. The temptation to spend money on clothes or a new phone may be strong, but those ‘wants’ will be replaced with newer wants, whereas, savings will help you achieve your goals and generate greater returns on investments.


Reach into Different Baskets

We are all looking for that one stock which will make us millions of dollars in the future, but that is highly unlikely and extremely risky. Have you heard the saying, “Diversify your investments.” Diversification is loved by everyone in the finance industry and is an extremely important concept in investing. When it comes to making money, never look for that “get rich quick” scheme, instead, get into the habit of diversifying. When combining a variety of investments, such as stocks, bonds, and mutual funds, you are reducing your exposure to risk. Both upside and downside potential is reduced when diversifying, however, it allows for a more consistent performance to your portfolio.


Opportunity Knocks

Most individuals would rather spend time on social media than to read a book, although getting into the habit of reading is very important when it comes to the world of investing. If you have invested or are investing your money, get familiar with reading the newspaper and other sources to get an idea of where your investment is headed. These sources will also provide you with a variety of new investing opportunities which have a potential for profits. Reading will allow you to set yourself up for the future, this way you can make accurate decisions about your investments and future goals.


The Long Game

The habit of dedication is what brings all the other habits together in order to put it all to work. As an investor, you can never let your money stay ‘still’. Investing regularly and on a consistent plan will keep your returns strong while keeping you in the game and caught up with the latest trends. Always review your portfolio and stay updated with your investments to ensure it continues to align with your goals. In investing and, for the matter, in every worthwhile endeavor in life, consistency breeds habit, and habit breeds success.
